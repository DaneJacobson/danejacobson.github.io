DeepMind's Alpha Series: Search+Learning on the path to AGI


I first started exploring machine learning around the time I switched from music to tech when Google DeepMind (GDM) released AlphaFold2. This post will summarize+document the major learnings from GDM's Alpha Series. This will also be a living document, as more models are built and I would not be surprised if the first AGI signs of life were discovered by the team at GDM given the infrastructure they've built around this approach.

The reason the Alpha Series is consistently named as such is due to one combined feature the AI systems all share: reinforcement learning (RL) + deep learning (DL). This tracks with The Bitter Lesson: search and learning are the two thrusts of machine intelligence research that scale with Moore's Law.

The Alpha Series of systems can be viewed as successive iterations or applications of the combination of search and learning, and it is my intent to describe here the:

  1. motivations
  2. core ideas
  3. domain applications

of each Alpha Series system. I will also, at the end of this post, describe what a final AGI system should look like based on the Alpha Series and if/where any non-DeepMind researchers/engineers can contribute to progress in this direction.

Lastly, if a member of GDM should read this and see flaws please don't hesitate to reach out to me via email or X and I will immediately make corrections. Thanks!
