Principles


Here are some things I believe. I express these ideas through quotes when I can because an author's body of work puts its weight behind a quote. If some of these rub you the wrong way - that's the point. Important truths should be controversial or unimportant to some people, otherwise there's a high likelihood you're not saying anything.

  • “Very often a man's whole life alters course because of a moment's hesitation” - Yukio Mishima, Spring Snow
  • Fear is your compass: do it now, do it faster, and never do the same thing twice
  • You can brew an adventure from a pot of nothing; there's a correlation between lifelong happiness and a willingness to just say "yes"; unhappiness is largely a function of regret and saying "no"
  • “There is no top, there are always further heights to reach” - Jascha Heifetz, 🎻🐐
  • “Competition is for losers” - Peter Thiel. Do not go gently into that Girardian night, whether in desires, interests, or philosophies
  • “Most people are other people” - Oscar Wilde, De Profundis
  • To understand anything, move up and down levels of abstraction rapidly
  • “Let’s find out!” - Francisco d’Anconia
  • “Chips on shoulders put chips in pockets” - Josh Wolfe
  • “The best things in life occur where the efficient market hypothesis is wrong” - Nat Friedman
  • Taste is like a seed. It's innate - and folks rarely let it bloom. But everyone has an inner Ziggy Stardust. Coax it out into the sunlight.
  • Consciousness is an emergent property of complexity and time-relative to the observing system. (I am working on proving this)
  • If you won’t die for anything, you’ll never truly live. Also, be friends with people who are alive.
